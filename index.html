Straight Talk

Common Hiring Mistakes (And How I Avoid Them)

I've seen what goes wrong. Here's how I'm different.

Over-Engineering

The Mistake: Building complex architectures for simple problems. Kubernetes for a blog.

My Approach: Right-sized solutions. I use the simplest technology that solves the problem reliably. Complexity is a cost, not a feature.

Ignoring SEO & Performance

The Mistake: Beautiful sites that nobody finds and nobody waits for.

My Approach: SEO and performance are first-class requirements. Semantic HTML, structured data, Core Web Vitals optimisation from day one.

Disappearing After Launch

The Mistake: Developers who vanish when bugs appear in production.

My Approach: I own what I build. Clear documentation, proper handoffs, and availability for post-launch support are standard.

Code Without Communication

The Mistake: Developers who deliver technically correct but wrong solutions.

My Approach: I ask questions early, provide regular updates, and surface blockers before they become disasters. No surprises.
